Customer Journey Map
Listing discrete parts of a customer's actions with corresponding swim lanes such as but not limited to thoughts, feelings, pain points, and opportunities.
Stakeholder Map
Listing the different stakeholders, their roles, and how they exchange value.
Service Blueprint
Similar to a customer journey map with customer actions divided into discrete steps on the top line, but with lines of interaction and backstage technologies/employee action in the swim lanes.
Challenges
Discerning what challenges the various parties face in completing their tasks.
Tasks
What do users want to accomplish and why? How do they go about accomplishing them?

Share Ideas
Get together with teammates and put all ideas on the table.
"Yes and ..." Thinking
Borrowed from improvisational comedy, this technique encourages ideas to flow between teammates.
Diverge
Create as many diverse ideas as possible with the idea that they'll eventually be winnowed down.
Reframe
Use techniues such as abstraction laddering, reverse assumptions, think outside the box for ways to solve your problem.
Prioritize
Now start converging on a solution based upon feasibility and product-market fit.

Varying Fidelities
Start with low-fidelity: the lack of personal investment will mean you're more likely to change it, plus the user is more likely to critique the purpose of the prototype and not details in its fit and finish.
Parallel Design
Design 3 prototypes for the same solution, conduct user tests, narrow your solution down to 2 potential solutions, conduct a second round of user tests, then select a final design. Studies show that parallel is more successful.
Fail Fast
By making iterations upon low-fidelity prototypes and testing them in the field, you'll quickly learn what works and what doesn't. Failing early saves both time and money later on.
Iterate Quickly
By adding small increments of value and testing them, you'll refine your prototype.

Think-Aloud Protocol
Now conduct a Think Aloud Protocol on your solution. See where users encounter difficulties with you app and make refinements based upon the insights you glean from this.
A/B Testing
Create two different versions of a feature and use data to validate which design leads to a more successful outcome.
Role Playing
Act as a customer; walk in their shoes and experience how they interact with your product or service.
Heuristic Evaluation
Have a UX practitioner go through your product and have them evaluate it against Jakob Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics for UI Design.
